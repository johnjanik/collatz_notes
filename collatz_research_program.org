** TODO [#B] Lyapunov exponent of F                                    :computation:analytical:
:PROPERTIES:
:Effort:   1w
:END:
The linearisation of F has matrix A = ((1/2, -1/2), (0, 3/2)) with
eigenvalues λ_s = 1/2 and λ_u = 3/2 (genuinely hyperbolic, area-
contracting by 3/4). Compute the Lyapunov exponent of the /random/
matrix product ∏ A_{σᵢ} where σᵢ is the branch label at step i.
Compare to log(3/2) and log(1/2).
- The hyperbolicity is a /random/ phenomenon (Furstenberg's theorem):
  the averaged transfer matrix ⟨M⟩ is parabolic, but fluctuations
  give positive Lyapunov exponent.
- Ref: Notes II §5.2, §7

* Key conjectures to test computationally
1. *Pseudo-Anosov type* (Notes II, Conj. 4.1): T_C on appropriate finite
   quotient of Σ_{2,3} has pseudo-Anosov type with unstable foliation slope
   log₂3 and stretch factor related to 3/2.

2. *Odometer conjugacy* (Notes II, Conj. 8.5): ∃ measurable φ conjugating
   F to a skew product over the 6-adic odometer.

3. *Collatz orbit measure ≠ Haar* (verified): D_q spectrum differs.
   Question: what is the exact multifractal f(α) spectrum?

4. *Parity SFT = golden mean shift* (verified, N = 5×10⁸): Topological
   skeleton is order-1 SFT with adjacency A = ((1,1),(1,0)), h_top = log₂φ.
   Invariant measure is sofic (not Parry), non-Markov at all finite orders.
   Entropy gap Δh ≈ 0.028 bits/step. See Notes I §12.

5. *Profinite compatibility* (verified, N = 10⁹): μ_k at 25 levels
   k = 2^a 3^b (0 ≤ a,b ≤ 4) form a consistent inverse system.
   All 200 marginal compatibility checks pass exactly.
   Correction: μ₃(1,1) is NOT forbidden (≈ N/9); the N=2×10⁶ zero was
   a finite-sample artifact. See Notes I §10.

6. *Odometer conjugacy* (Notes II, Conj. 8.5): ∃ measurable φ conjugating
   F to a skew product over the 6-adic odometer.
   Partial negative result: 6-adic ordering shows no privileged local constancy
   vs 5-adic/7-adic controls (Notes I §11).

7. *Galois-dynamical correspondence* (Notes II, speculative): ŵ(n)
   transforms under cyclotomic character.
